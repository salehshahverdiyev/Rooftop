When will I get the property?

The time to acquire the property will depend on several factors of the process. These can be factors such as the type of property, location, agreements between the seller and the buyer, credit processes of banks, and so on. Each property purchase process can be different and therefore it is difficult to set a specific time frame. Your estate agent can contact you with more detailed information about the stages and timeframes in the property acquisition process. Your agent will guide you through the process of collecting the necessary documents, notarization, banking and other procedures and guide you through the process of acquiring the property. The foreclosure process can generally take several weeks or months. In this process, we will carefully monitor all the steps necessary to acquire your property and keep you informed at every stage. However, information may vary depending on your specific property buying experience and local laws. Your agent will provide you with complete information and guide you through the process of acquiring your property, showing you the best measures to get the best.

What are the additional costs of the property?

Property charges refer to other charges that come with the property you buy or rent. Along with a working property, the following additional costs are the most common: Collection and property taxes: When you own a property, it is your responsibility to pay the taxes and fees imposed by the local courts. This varies depending on the value, area and location of the property. Heat, water, electricity and other utilities: During the period you use the property, you must also arrange the utility bills that customers are billed for basic services. This may include services such as heating system, water, electricity, internet, telephone and others. Repair and maintenance costs: You may incur costs for corrective repairs and maintenance to maintain the quality and value of your property. This can be painting, building maintenance, yard maintenance, water extraction and other maintenance and renovation work. Property Insurance: You may want to insure your property for your safety. This may involve paying for an insurance policy which may vary depending on the value of the property and the risks involved. Management and property agency services: When you want to rent out your property, you need to contact property agencies or management companies and pay them for their services. This may include services such as property management, rental agreement drafting, tenant selection, property advertising, and more.

How does the property sales process work?

Property valuation: As a first step, the value of the property must be determined. The valuation process is based on factors related to the property's characteristics, location, title deeds and the surrounding property market. Advertising and marketing: An effective advertising strategy should be set up to showcase the property for sale. This is done by disseminating property information on websites, social media platforms, kitchens, brochures and more. Meetings and discussions: Meetings are arranged with potential buyers and they are presented with the details, features and terms of sale of the property. Buyers' questions are answered and price is negotiated. Agreement and documentation: Once an agreement is reached with the buyer, a written agreement is entered into that covers the terms for the sale of the property. In this agreement, the sale price of the property, terms of payment, rights and responsibilities of the buyer and the seller should be specified in sufficient detail. Legal procedures and registration: Legal procedures must be followed to confirm the sale agreement. This may be related to notarization, payment of property debts with organizations, execution of tasks and application of changes. This process ensures that the sale is legally complete.

Mortgage information and what to look out for

Mortgage refers to the process of obtaining a housing loan for the purchase of a property. Mortgages are usually long-term and large loans that give the buyer the right to own the part of the property (for example, a house, apartment) that the owner owns. A working mortgage process consists of the following stages: Credit eligibility check: The first step is to check the loan application by the bank. The bank will evaluate the buyer's financial strength and eligibility for the loan, check the credit score, income and debts. Discussion of mortgage conditions: The mortgage conditions provided by the bank are discussed. These conditions include factors such as loan interest, repayment period, advance amount, property price and others. Property valuation: The bank determines the price of the property and determines the amount of the mortgage loan based on it. In this process, the value of the property is evaluated based on market value and development prospects. Liaison with Institutions: Liaises with property registry and government institutions to verify ownership documents and other legal procedures to fulfill the mortgage request. Closing the deal and loan approval: The mortgage agreement is signed by the bank and the buyer and the legal procedures for loan approval are followed. In this process, the buyer accepts payment arrangements and other mortgage terms. The mortgage process can be complex and detailed. Before starting the mortgage process, buyers need to gather accurate information and consider the following points: Loan interest: The interest rate offered by the bank and its long-term effect. Repayment terms: Detailed information about the loan repayment period, monthly payments and penalty interest. Advance Amount: The minimum advance amount required to issue a mortgage loan.
